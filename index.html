HELLO WRLD – Miniature Surreal Universes

HELLO WRLD is a daring hybrid microseries compressed into 60–90 second episodes. Each clip begins in a familiar, mundane space — a street corner, a stairwell, a living room — and then fractures, revealing a hidden surreal layer, as if reality itself was glitching. Everyday environments are volumetrically scanned, processed as Gaussian splats, and staged inside Unreal Engine 5, where real-time rendering blends with stop-motion, motion capture, and VFX. The result: miniature, poetic universes that balance humor, absurdity, and magical realism.

The project has two goals: (1) to create a series of experimental short films for festivals and digital platforms, and (2) to test and document a scalable production pipeline using UE5 for volumetric storytelling, real-time collage, and Gaussian splat integration. With MegaGrant support, we aim to prototype 2–3 episodes and establish the technical foundation for a full season of 12 episodes, adaptable across vertical (social/web), horizontal (streaming), and XR formats.

Creator

She is a Creative XR Producer and Animator with a BA in Animation from HSLU. With a background in audiovisual media and design, she works at the intersection of storytelling, art, and technology. Additionally, she curates and produces exhibitions and festivals, both nationally and internationally.
